Yoruba Culture Story

The Talking Drum and the Wise King

Long ago in the ancient Yoruba city of Oyo, there was a king known as Alaafin Sango, famous for his wisdom and power over lightning. In his palace was a gifted drummer named Ayan, whose talking drum (gángan) could send messages across the land.

One day, a far-off village faced danger from wild animals, but there was no time to send a messenger on foot. Ayan took his talking drum, tightened its strings, and began to play. The drum spoke in Yoruba tones, carrying the message over hills and forests:

“The king calls! Danger is near! Come together!”

Within minutes, warriors from surrounding villages arrived, ready to protect the people. The wild animals were driven away, and peace returned.

From that day, the people respected the talking drum not just as a musical instrument, but as a voice of unity and tradition.
